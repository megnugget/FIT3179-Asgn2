CANCER

- A WIDESPREAD DISEASE -

CANCER [kan-ser]
noun

"A serious disease that is caused when cells in the body grow in a way that is uncontrolled and not normal, killing normal cells and often causing death"             - Cambridge Dictionary

MAIN CAUSE OF DEATH

Cancer is one of the world's largest killer. According to The Global Burden of Disease, the number of deaths by cancer in 2017 ranks second after cardiovascular diseases.

HOW MANY PEOPLE HAVE CANCER ?

Overall, we can see that the prevalence of cancer is higher in more developed countries such as USA, Canada, and Australia . This is because the population is associated with more affluent lifestyles that leads to cancer such as smoking and alcohol consumption.

AGE AND CANCER

Mortality rates are strongly related to age. Elderlies with the age 70 and above has the highest number of deaths. This is due to the possibility of long-term cell damage. As we become older, this damage can build up and result in cancer.

MORTALITY RATE BY TYPE OF CANCER

Tracheal, bronchus, and lung cancer are the deadliest cancers worldwide, accounting for 1.9 million deaths in 2017. The second most deadly form of cancer is colorectum cancer, followed by stomach and breast cancer.
